Welcome to the Neighborhood

When Longfellow Real Estate Partners came to Mariners Island in 2021 with a $22 million redevelopment project, the community engaged constructively and offered specific technical guidance to prevent noise problems. This is what happened instead.

‚úì What Longfellow DID

  • Ground equipment relocated to Baker Way
  • 18-foot sound walls built
  • "Whisper quiet" equipment specified
  • Acoustic treatment installed
  • Result: ~48 dB(A) at property line

‚úó What Longfellow DID NOT DO

  • ZERO acoustic treatment for rooftop equipment
  • No enclosures, barriers, or mitigation
  • Installed exactly where community warned against
  • Claims compliance using flawed methodology
  • Result: Residents experience predicted noise

The Timeline

January 2022

Community warned: Rooftop HVAC will project noise to residences. Requested equipment be located away from homes.

June 2024

Prediction came true: Residents file complaints reporting exactly what was predicted 2.5 years earlier.

July 2024

Inadequate response: One-page memo claiming compliance using ground-level measurements that miss rooftop impact.

204+ Days

Still waiting: No meaningful mitigation. No commitment to address rooftop equipment.

The Real Issue: Measurement Methodology

The Problem: Ground-level measurements with dB(A)-only weighting systematically underestimate actual impact by 15-20 dB.

  • Wrong location: Measured at ground, but rooftop sources ‚Üí upper-floor residences
  • Wrong weighting: dB(A) underestimates 63 Hz by 26 dB
  • No penalties: No tonal or low-frequency adjustments applied

Result: Claims ~48 dB(A) compliance while residents experience 65-70 dB with proper assessment
