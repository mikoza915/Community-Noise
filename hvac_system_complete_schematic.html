Why Rooftop Equipment Creates the Problem:

  • Elevation Advantage: Equipment 30-40 feet above grade projects sound over long distances with minimal attenuation
  • Clear Line-of-Sight: No natural or man-made barriers between rooftop equipment and upper-floor residences
  • Elevated-to-Elevated Path: Sound travels horizontally from rooftop to upper-floor windows with no ground interaction or barrier effect
  • 24/7 Operation: Lab climate control requires constant operation - no "quiet hours"
  • Multiple Sources: Cumulative effect of AHUs, cooling towers, and exhaust fans operating simultaneously
  • Low-Frequency Dominance: HVAC equipment produces 31.5-250 Hz content that travels farther and penetrates buildings more effectively
  • Tonal Character: Fan blade pass frequencies and compressor harmonics create detectable pure tones
