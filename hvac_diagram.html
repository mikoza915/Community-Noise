Major Components and Noise Characteristics

1. Chiller Unit (88-95 dB @ 3 feet)

Function: Removes heat from water circulation loop to provide chilled water for air handlers

Noise Sources:

  • Compressor (85-95 dB): Scroll or screw type, 150-300 kW power draw
    • Dominant frequencies: 60 Hz (motor), 120 Hz (2x motor frequency)
    • Strong low-frequency tonal components
    • Mechanical vibration transmitted through mounting structure
  • Condenser (80-88 dB): Heat rejection through air-cooled or water-cooled process
    • Fan noise (broadband with blade pass frequency tone)
    • Refrigerant flow noise
‚ö†Ô∏è Why This Matters: The 60 Hz and 120 Hz tones are exactly what residents describe as "constant hum." A-weighting applies -26 dB correction at 63 Hz, severely underestimating annoyance.

2. Cooling Tower (82-90 dB - Water-Cooled Systems Only)

Function: Rejects heat from condenser water to atmosphere via evaporation

Noise Sources:

  • Fan (82-90 dB): Large propeller fan, 20-50 kW
    • Broadband "whoosh" with blade pass frequency tone
    • Low-frequency dominated (31.5-250 Hz)
  • Water (75-80 dB): Water falling onto fill material
    • Broadband splashing noise

3. Air Handling Units / AHU (75-85 dB)

Function: Circulate conditioned air through building, contain filters, cooling coils, fans

Noise Sources:

  • Supply/Return Fans: 30-75 kW each
    • Centrifugal blower noise
    • Blade pass frequency tones
    • Air turbulence in ductwork

4. Pumps (70-80 dB)

Function: Circulate chilled water between chiller and air handlers

Noise Sources:

  • Motor noise (60 Hz fundamental)
  • Impeller/cavitation noise
  • Pipe vibration and water flow noise

5. Exhaust Fans (72-82 dB)

Function: Exhaust stale air, bring in fresh air

Noise Sources:

  • Fan motor and blade noise
  • Air velocity through louvers

Combined Noise Effects

Sound Addition (Not Simple Addition!)

Multiple noise sources do NOT add arithmetically. Sound levels combine logarithmically:

  • Two identical 90 dB sources = 93 dB (not 180 dB!)
  • Four identical 90 dB sources = 96 dB
  • 90 dB + 80 dB = 90.4 dB (quieter source barely contributes)

Practical Result: Combined rooftop HVAC system typically 3-6 dB higher than loudest single component.

Distance Attenuation

Inverse Square Law

Sound decreases by approximately 6 dB per doubling of distance in open field conditions:

  • At 10 feet: 90 dB
  • At 20 feet: 84 dB (-6 dB)
  • At 40 feet: 78 dB (-6 dB)
  • At 80 feet: 72 dB (-6 dB)
  • At 160 feet: 66 dB (-6 dB)
  • At 320 feet: 60 dB (-6 dB)
  • At 640 feet: 54 dB (-6 dB)
‚ö†Ô∏è Real-World Factors:
  • Ground effect: Can reduce attenuation at low frequencies
  • Wind: Downwind propagation can extend noise farther
  • Temperature inversion: Nighttime conditions can focus sound toward ground
  • Multiple sources: Several pieces of equipment reduce effective attenuation
  • Reflections: Nearby buildings can reflect sound, reducing attenuation

Result: Actual attenuation often less than ideal -6 dB/doubling, especially at night and for low frequencies.

Why Low-Frequency Noise is Problematic

‚ö†Ô∏è The 60 Hz Problem

Motor fundamental frequency: 60 Hz (AC line frequency)

  • A-weighting correction at 63 Hz: -26 dB
    • Actual 80 dB at 63 Hz ‚Üí Measured as 54 dB(A)
    • Severely underestimates human annoyance
  • Wall transmission: Low frequencies penetrate building envelopes easily
    • High frequencies: -30 to -40 dB through walls
    • Low frequencies: -15 to -20 dB through walls
  • Room resonances: Indoor standing waves can amplify low frequencies by +10 to +20 dB at certain locations
  • Tonal character: Pure tones 5-10 dB more annoying than broadband noise

Result: 60 Hz motor hum is exactly what creates "I can hear it but can't measure it" situations when only A-weighted measurements are used.

San Mateo Science Center Application

The San Mateo Science Center (Buildings 901 and 951) operates a system very similar to this diagram:

  • Rooftop chillers: Operating 24/7 for laboratory climate control
  • Distance to Harbortown residences: ~620-750 feet
  • Expected attenuation: 90 dB at source ‚Üí ~54-58 dB at residences (before penalties)
  • With penalties:
    • +5 dB low-frequency (C-A differential > 10 dB)
    • +5 dB tonal (63 Hz motor hum)
    • Adjusted level: 64-68 dB(A)
  • San Mateo limit (Zone 2 nighttime): 50 dB(A)
  • Violation: 14-18 dB over limit (when properly assessed)
